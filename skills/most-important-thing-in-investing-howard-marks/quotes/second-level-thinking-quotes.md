# The Most Important Thing — Second-Level Thinking Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Most Important Thing Illuminated* (Howard Marks, 2013).

---

## second-level-defined

> "Second-level thinking is deep, complex, and contrarian. It involves thinking about what others are thinking, and what they're likely to do."

*(Chapter 1: Second-Level Thinking)*

---

## first-vs-second

> "First-level thinking says: 'I think the company's earnings will fall; let's sell.' Second-level thinking says: 'I think the company's earnings will fall less than people expect, and the pleasant surprise will lift the stock; let's buy.'"

*(Chapter 1: Second-Level Thinking)*

---

## outperformance-requires-difference

> "You can't do the same things others do and expect to outperform."

*(Chapter 11: Contrarianism)*

---

## be-wrong-look-foolish

> "You must be willing to be wrong and look foolish."

*(Chapter 11: Contrarianism)*

---

## market-efficiency-limitations

> "Markets are mostly efficient, but inefficiencies exist. The key is finding areas where efficiency breaks down."

*(Chapter 2: Understanding Market Efficiency)*

---

## inefficiency-sources

> "Inefficiency arises from lack of information, analytical errors, or behavioral biases."

*(Chapter 2: Understanding Market Efficiency)*

---

## unconventional-behavior

> "Superior investment results can only be achieved by doing something different from what others are doing."

*(Chapter 1: Second-Level Thinking)*

---

## thinking-differently

> "The difference between first-level and second-level thinking is the difference between ordinary and extraordinary results."

*(Chapter 1: Second-Level Thinking)*
