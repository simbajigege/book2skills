# The Most Important Thing — Value Investing Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Most Important Thing Illuminated* (Howard Marks, 2013).

---

## second-level-thinking

> "First-level thinking says: 'It's a good company; let's buy the stock.' Second-level thinking says: 'It's a good company, but everyone thinks it's a great company, and it's not. So the stock's overpriced; let's sell.'"

*(Chapter 1: Second-Level Thinking)*

---

## no-asset-so-good

> "No asset is so good it can't become overpriced, and few are so bad they can't get cheap."

*(Chapter 4: The Relationship Between Price and Value)*

---

## price-determines-return

> "The price paid determines the return."

*(Chapter 4: The Relationship Between Price and Value)*

---

## value-investing-defined

> "Value investing means buying at a discount to intrinsic value."

*(Chapter 3: Value)*

---

## finding-bargains

> "Bargains arise from misunderstanding, neglect, and forced selling. The best opportunities come where few are looking."

*(Chapter 12: Finding Bargains)*

---

## patient-opportunism

> "You don't always need to be fully invested. Wait for the right pitch. Most of the time, the best thing to do is nothing."

*(Chapter 13: Patient Opportunism)*

---

## contrarianism-essential

> "You cannot do the same things others do and expect to outperform."

*(Chapter 11: Contrarianism)*

---

## this-time-is-never-different

> "Feeling that 'this time it's different.' It's never different."

*(Chapter 10: Combating Negative Influences)*

---

## know-what-you-dont-know

> "Acknowledge the limits of your knowledge. Forecasting is unreliable."

*(Chapter 14: Knowing What You Don't Know)*

---

## no-single-most-important-thing

> "The 'most important thing' is that there is no single most important thing — you need to address them all."

*(Chapter 21: Pulling It All Together)*
