# The Most Important Thing — Market Cycles Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Most Important Thing Illuminated* (Howard Marks, 2013).

---

## everything-cycles

> "Everything cycles. Markets swing between extremes; the trend will not continue forever."

*(Chapter 8: Being Attuned to Cycles)*

---

## pendulum-swings

> "Market psychology swings between optimism and pessimism. Understanding pendulum position is key to contrarian action."

*(Chapter 9: Awareness of the Pendulum)*

---

## six-negative-influences

> "Six emotions that hurt investors — greed, fear, suspension of disbelief, conformity, envy, and capitulation."

*(Chapter 10: Combating Negative Influences)*

---

## where-we-stand

> "You need to know where you are in the market cycle to calibrate your actions. Assess the 'temperature' of the market."

*(Chapter 15: Having a Sense for Where We Stand)*

---

## luck-not-skill

> "Much of investment success comes from luck, not skill. Don't confuse luck with skill, and don't overestimate your abilities."

*(Chapter 16: Appreciating the Role of Luck)*

---

## reasonable-expectations

> "You can't achieve above-average returns without taking above-average risk. But you shouldn't take above-average risk expecting above-average returns — that's a recipe for disappointment."

*(Chapter 20: Reasonable Expectations)*

---

## survival-first

> "Focus on survival first."

*(Chapter 17: Investing Defensively)*

---

## good-times-teach-bad-lessons

> "Good times teach only bad lessons: that investing is easy, that you know its secrets, and that you needn't worry about risk. The most valuable lessons are learned in tough times."

*(Introduction)*
