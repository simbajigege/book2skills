# Essays in Persuasion - Demand and Confidence Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *Essays in Persuasion* (John Maynard Keynes).

---

## slump-trade-employment

> "The slump in trade and employment and the business losses which are being incurred are as bad as the worst which have ever occurred in the modern history of the world."

*(Saving and Spending)*

---

## idle-workers-worldwide

> "In the three chief industrial countries of the world, Great Britain, Germany, and the United States, I estimate that probably 12,000,000 industrial workers stand idle."

*(Saving and Spending)*

---

## public-works-remedy

> "Several of the Committee's recommendations, e.g. those relating to Roads, to Housing, and to Afforestation, do indeed expressly imply that the whole theory underlying the principle of Public Works as a remedy for unemployment is mistaken."

*(The Economy Report)*

---

## doing-spending-enterprises

> "We can all the same do something by ourselves and that something must take the form of activity, of doing things, of spending, of setting great enterprises afoot."

*(Saving and Spending)*

---

## restore-confidence-enterprise

> "In every way the most effective remedy would be that the Central Banks of these three great creditor nations should join together in a bold scheme to restore confidence to the international long-term loan market."

*(The Great Slump of 1930)*

---

## revive-enterprise-activity

> "Which would serve to revive enterprise and activity everywhere, and to restore prices and profits, so that in due course the wheels of the world's commerce would go round again."

*(The Great Slump of 1930)*

---

## deflation-transfers-wealth

> "Deflation, as we have already seen, involves a transference of wealth from the rest of the community to the rentier class and to all holders of titles to money."

*(The Return to Gold)*

---

## active-to-inactive

> "In particular it involves a transference from all borrowers, that is to say from traders, manufacturers, and farmers, to lenders, from the active to the inactive."

*(The Return to Gold)*
