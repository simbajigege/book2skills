# Creating Superfans — Exceeding Expectations Extracts

Verbatim excerpts from *Creating Superfans: Turning Everyday Customers into Enthusiastic Advocates* (Brittany Hodak, 2023).

---

## experience-matters

> "80% of customers said the experience a company provides matters as much as its products or services."

*(Chapter 6: My List — Salesforce Research)*

---

## willingness-to-pay-more

> "Up to 86% of customers are willing to pay more money for a great customer experience."

*(Chapter 1: Where It's At — PwC)*

---

## small-things-matter

> "Small moments of surprise and delight compound over time."

> "Consistency matters more than grand gestures."

*(Chapter 11: All the Small Things)*

---

## before-and-after-moments

> "Customers shouldn't only hear from you when you're back to ask for more money (for example, when it's time for renewals, or there's a premium increase, or you're responding to a submitted claim)."

> "'Before' is your chance to set yourself apart from every competitor out there by sharing your story."

> "The 'after' is critical because it's so much easier and cheaper to sell something to someone again than it is to sell something to a new customer for the first time. It's also where all the relational gold happens."

*(Chapter 12: Hanging by a Moment)*

---

## one-repeatable-action

> "One repeatable, memorable action that triggers an experience for your customer at the end of every transaction can mean the difference between a one-time customer and a lifelong superfan."

*(Chapter 12: Hanging by a Moment)*

---

## jim-harbaugh-example

> "The moment I received that note, I became a University of Michigan football superfan. One card was more effective at turning me into a loyal Wolverine than all the years I'd spent going to Michigan games and tailgates and pep rallies, because it connected my story to the team's story."

*(Chapter 3: Brand New Set of Rules)*

---

## handwritten-notes-power

> "There is no such thing as too busy—only too uninterested."

> "On the first point, it is possible to send handwritten notes at scale. There are plenty of tech solutions, AI and otherwise, that automate outreach."

> "On the second point, you don't have to be a celebrity to make an impact. The power lies in showing people you care about them by taking the time to acknowledge them in some way."

*(Chapter 3: Brand New Set of Rules)*

---

## service-recovery-paradox

> "The term 'service recovery paradox' was first used in 1992 to describe the phenomenon where customers who experience a service failure that is exceptionally well-handled can end up being more satisfied and loyal than customers who never experienced a problem in the first place."

*(Chapter 13: Back 2 Good)*

---

## harbaugh-consistency

> "He's not some untouchable celebrity whose schedule is too packed to acknowledge people; he's a man of character who leads by example, even when no one is watching."

*(Chapter 3: Brand New Set of Rules)*

---

## every-interaction-matters

> "Every employee is the most important ambassador of the company. Every interaction is the one with the potential to shape the company's reputation for the next decade."

*(Chapter 8: Read My Mind)*

---

## measuring-experience

> "You can't know for sure if you're exceeding customers' expectations unless you measure their feedback in some way."

> "My two favorite metrics for measuring customer experience are CSAT, which happens in the moment or immediately after an interaction, and EGR, which is a measure of the revenue coming from repeat and referred customers over time."

*(Chapter 14: Here I Go Again)*

---

## create-experiences-worth-talking-about

> "Customers rarely, if ever, tell others about an average experience with a product or service—just as people seldom comment on a forgettable song they heard on the radio."

> "These things are filler, helping us pass the time between Point A and Point B. Too many businesses fall into the trap of becoming something lots of people 'sort of' like instead of intentionally creating an experience the right people can't stop telling their friends about."

> "Don't be filler; be unforgettable."

*(Chapter 2: I Don't Care)*
