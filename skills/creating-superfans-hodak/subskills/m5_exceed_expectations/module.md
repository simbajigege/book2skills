# Skill: Exceed Expectations (Creating Superfans - Module 5)

**Knowledge source:** *Creating Superfans* by Brittany Hodak.
**Reference library:** `references/case_library.md`
**Standalone use:** This module can run independently or after M1, M3, and M4.

## When to Use

Use this module when the user asks how to wow customers, create word of mouth, increase referrals, design memorable experiences, improve onboarding or post-purchase moments, or make an ordinary journey worth talking about.

## Module Inputs

- Customer journey or touchpoints
- Current customer expectations
- Emotional, uncertain, inconvenient, or memorable moments
- Current follow-up, referral, review, renewal, or post-purchase process
- Operational constraints

## Module Outputs

Return an experience design plan with:
- Key expectation gap
- Before and after moments
- Repeatable memorable action
- Advocacy trigger
- Measurement method

## Execution Flow

### Overview

```text
Map expectations -> find emotional moments -> design small repeatable exceeders -> connect to advocacy -> measure memory and behavior
```

### Step 1: Map current expectations

**Core question:** What does the customer expect at each touchpoint, and where is the brand merely adequate?

**Decision rules:**

| Signal | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| Customers say "fine" but do not return or refer | Adequate but not memorable |
| Follow-up only asks for payment, renewal, review, or upsell | Relationship is transactional |
| Brand compares only to direct competitors | Experience bar is too low |

**Ask the user:** Which touchpoint is most emotional, uncertain, inconvenient, or memorable?

**Citation instruction:** When explaining why experience matters, cite `#experience-matters` or `#every-interaction-matters`.

### Step 2: Design before and after moments

**Core question:** Which moments before and after the transaction can create memory?

**Decision rules:**

| Moment | Design question |
|--------|-----------------|
| Before purchase | How can expectations be clarified or anxiety reduced? |
| During purchase | What small care signal proves the story? |
| After purchase | What ending or follow-up would customers remember? |
| After success | What advocacy trigger would feel natural? |

**Ask the user:** What happens immediately before and after the customer receives value?

**Citation instruction:** For before/after journey design, cite `#before-and-after-moments`.

### Step 3: Make it repeatable and talk-worthy

**Core question:** What small action can be repeated without operational chaos?

**Decision rules:**

| Good action | Bad action |
|-------------|------------|
| Low-cost, high-attention, customer-centered | Expensive, generic, or brand-centered |
| Easy to train and repeat | Depends on one heroic person |
| Creates a story customers can retell | Is invisible to the customer |

**Ask the user:** What would a customer voluntarily tell a friend about this moment?

**Citation instruction:** When selecting the action, cite `#small-things-matter`, `#one-repeatable-action`, or `#create-experiences-worth-talking-about`.

## Do Not

- Do not advise vague delight or wow tactics.
- Do not recommend grand gestures before fixing broken basics.
- Do not assume no complaints means a remarkable experience.
- Do not create one-off moments that cannot be repeated.
