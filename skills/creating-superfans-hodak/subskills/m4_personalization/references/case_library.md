# Creating Superfans — Personalization Extracts

Verbatim excerpts from *Creating Superfans: Turning Everyday Customers into Enthusiastic Advocates* (Brittany Hodak, 2023).

---

## platinum-rule

> "The Platinum Rule takes the Golden Rule a step further: Treat others the way THEY want to be treated."

> "If you treat other people the way you want to be treated, you're assuming their preferences are the same as yours. But they may not be."

*(Chapter 9: I Want You to Want Me — Tony Alessandra)*

---

## personalization-expectations

> "71% of consumers expect companies to deliver personalized interactions. And 76% get frustrated when that doesn't happen."

*(Chapter 9: I Want You to Want Me — McKinsey & Company)*

---

## personalization-at-scale

> "Personalization doesn't mean creating a bespoke experience for every customer. But it does mean acknowledging what makes each customer unique and using that information to make them feel seen and understood."

*(Chapter 9: I Want You to Want Me)*

---

## gifting-principles

> "The right gift at the right time is always impactful, regardless of its cost."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## think-outside-the-office

> "Even though we're all grown-ups now, sometimes we forget that the prospects and customers we're trying so hard to create relationships with exist outside of their relationship with us. They've got interests, hobbies, and passions outside of work."

> "You don't have to go with a gift that sits on or around a desk (or wherever it is that they do their work)."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## acknowledge-family

> "Don't just show your appreciation to customers or prospects. Spread the love with the ones they love. It's not only good manners to acknowledge these supporting players, but also good business."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## gifts-not-ads

> "If you're giving something with your logo on it, it's an ad, not a gift. You're asking them to advertise their connection to your company, which makes it transactional: 'Enjoy this while you promote me for free!'"

> "While you would never do this in a personal context, companies do it all the time. Don't be one of them."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## make-it-unique

> "Even though gifts should be about the recipient, it's okay to connect your story to theirs in a meaningful way. In fact, it's more than okay because, as you absolutely know by now, superfans are created at the intersection of your story and every customer's story."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## mark-important-dates

> "I'm a proponent of acknowledging birthdays and anniversaries if for no other reason than because, when you don't, your absence can be noticed."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## half-birthday-opportunity

> "If you know your client's birthday, you know their half-birthday, too. Send them a quick card or email acknowledging the day. There's a good chance they may not even realize it's their half-birthday... which means you could make their day that much brighter."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## personalization-exceeds-expectations

> "John's blender gift was SUPER because:
> • It aligned with his story, which is rooted in selfless generosity (S).
> • It demonstrated that he was listening and understood a need (U).
> • It was clearly personalized to connect at the right time (P).
> • It was a surprise, thus exceeding non-existent expectations (E).
> • Finally, John is always looking for these kinds of opportunities (R)."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*

---

## CRM-for-personalization

> "This is an example of where customer relationship management (CRM) software comes into play in a big way. Even if some detail doesn't seem important now (for example, a client mentioned that his wife is going to Cancún on a girls' trip next month), you may want to add it to the Notes section of your CRM."

*(Chapter 8: Read My Mind)*

---

## small-personal-touches

> "It doesn't take a $600 blender to make an impression on someone. I was thrilled to receive the blender, but I was also touched every time an employee or coworker noticed I worked through lunch and surprised me with a Dr Pepper and tacos at 3 p.m."

*(Chapter 10: You Get What You Give)*
