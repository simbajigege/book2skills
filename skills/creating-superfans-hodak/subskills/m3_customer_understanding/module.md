# Skill: Customer Understanding (Creating Superfans - Module 3)

**Knowledge source:** *Creating Superfans* by Brittany Hodak.
**Reference library:** `references/case_library.md`
**Standalone use:** This module can run independently or provide upstream context for M4, M5, M6, and M7.

## When to Use

Use this module when the user asks who their customers are, why customers buy, what customers want, how to improve messaging, how to sell better, or why customers churn despite apparent satisfaction.

## Module Inputs

- Customer segment or persona
- Current customer research, reviews, support tickets, churn notes, or sales calls
- Product or service use case
- Desired business outcome such as retention, referrals, conversion, or recovery

## Module Outputs

Return a customer STORY map with:
- Struggles
- Transformation
- Outcomes
- Relationships
- Why this brand
- Voice-of-customer evidence and follow-up questions

## Execution Flow

### Overview

```text
Collect customer evidence -> apply STORY lens -> separate assumptions from evidence -> convert product language into transformation language
```

### Step 1: Apply the STORY model

**Core question:** What is the customer's story, not the company's sales script?

**Decision rules:**

| STORY element | What to capture |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Struggles | Pain, friction, uncertainty, fear, inconvenience |
| Transformation | What life or work should look like after success |
| Outcomes | Tangible results, status, relief, speed, confidence |
| Relationships | People affected by the decision |
| Why you | Why this brand is credible for this customer |

**Ask the user:** What exact words do customers use when describing the problem?

**Citation instruction:** When introducing this model, cite `#story-model-for-customers`.

### Step 2: Practice empathy before authority

**Core question:** Has the brand shown that it understands the customer before prescribing?

**Decision rules:**

| Strong signal | Warning signal |
|---------------|----------------|
| The brand reflects the customer's words and concerns | The brand leads with expertise, credentials, or specs |
| Sales and support ask follow-up questions | Scripts prevent discovery |
| Customer feedback changes behavior | Feedback is collected but ignored |

**Ask the user:** Where in the journey does the brand prove it listened?

**Citation instruction:** When pushing empathy before authority, cite `#empathy-precedes-authority` or `#understand-before-selling`.

### Step 3: Convert evidence into action

**Core question:** How should customer understanding change the next touchpoint?

**Decision rules:**

| Evidence source | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| Reviews | Extract repeated phrases and frustrations |
| Support tickets | Identify recurring expectations and failure moments |
| Sales calls | Capture transformation language and objections |
| Churn notes | Identify adoption gaps or broken promises |

**Ask the user:** What is one recent customer conversation that changed what you thought?

**Citation instruction:** When advising research and listening, cite `#voice-of-customer` or `#active-listening-five-steps`.

## Do Not

- Do not recommend tactics from product-centered assumptions.
- Do not treat demographics as customer understanding.
- Do not lead with authority before empathy.
- Do not ignore actual customer language when it is available.
