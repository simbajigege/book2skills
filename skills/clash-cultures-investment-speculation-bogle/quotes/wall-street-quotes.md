# The Clash of the Cultures — Wall Street & Financial System Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Clash of the Cultures* (John C. Bogle, 2012).

---

## casino-wall-street

> "I don't see any difference between Las Vegas Boulevard and Wall Street: Over time we can't lose, but there will be games where we take a hit."

—Senior executive of Cantor Fitzgerald, 2010

*(Chapter 1 — The Wall Street Casino)*

---

## socially-useless-activity

> "Much of what happens in the world's financial centers [is] 'socially useless activity.'"

—Lord Adair Turner, chairman of Britain's Financial Services Authority

*(Chapter 1 — Creating Value versus Subtracting Value)*

---

## adding-vs-subtracting-value

> "It is surely one of the great paradoxes of the day that the largest financial rewards in our nation are received by an investment community that subtracts value from its clients, with far smaller rewards received by a business community that adds value to society."

*(Chapter 1 — Compensation Issues)*

---

## agency-society-reversal

> "In 1950, individual investors held 92 percent of U.S. stocks and institutional investors held 8 percent. The roles have flipped, with institutions, now holding 70 percent, predominating, and individuals, now holding 30 percent, playing a secondary role."

*(Chapter 1 — How Speculation Overwhelmed Investment)*

---

## kaufman-on-financial-amnesia

> "Today's financial community is suffering from a bad case of amnesia. Most Wall Streeters are unaware of or have forgotten about the damaging effects of irresponsible behavior in their rush to 'innovate' and profit."

—Henry Kaufman, 2001

*(Chapter 1 — Box 1.3)*

---

## kaufman-on-fiduciary-duty

> "People in finance are entrusted with an extraordinary responsibility: other people's money. This basic fiduciary duty too often has been forgotten in the high-voltage, high-velocity financial environment that has emerged in recent decades."

—Henry Kaufman, 2001

*(Chapter 1 — Box 1.3)*

---

## derivatives-magnitude

> "Trading in S&P 500-linked futures totaled more than $60 trillion(!) in 2011, five times the S&P 500 Index total market capitalization of $12.5 trillion."

*(Chapter 1 — Futures and Derivatives)*

---

## liquidity-last-refuge

> "Paraphrasing Samuel Johnson on patriotism, I wonder if liquidity hasn't become 'the last refuge of the scoundrel.'"

*(Chapter 1 — High-Frequency Trading)*

---

## keynes-prediction-on-professionals

> "Expert professionals, possessing judgment and knowledge beyond that of the average private investor, would become concerned, not with making superior long-term forecasts of the probable yield on an investment over its entire life, but with forecasting changes in the conventional valuation a short time ahead of the general public."

—John Maynard Keynes, 1936

*(Chapter 1 — Speculation Will Crowd Out Investment)*
