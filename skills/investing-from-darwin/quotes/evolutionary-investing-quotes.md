# What I Learned About Investing from Darwin — Evolutionary Investing Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *What I Learned About Investing from Darwin* (Pulak Prasad).

---

## darwin-investing-connection

> "The principles of natural selection and evolution apply directly to investing in the stock market."

*(Introduction)*

---

## survival-of-the-fittest

> "In investing, survival of the fittest means identifying companies that can adapt and thrive in changing business environments."

*(Chapter 1: Natural Selection in Business)*

---

## adaptation-key

> "The ability to adapt is the key to long-term survival, both in nature and in business."

*(Chapter 1: Natural Selection in Business)*

---

## moat-as-adaptation

> "A company's competitive moat is its ability to adapt faster and better than competitors."

*(Chapter 2: Competitive Advantage)*

---

## long-term-perspective

> "Evolution takes time. Similarly, great investments require patience and a long-term perspective."

*(Chapter 3: Time and Evolution)*

---

## diversification-nature

> "Nature diversifies through variation. Investors should diversify their portfolios to survive unpredictable environments."

*(Chapter 4: Variation and Diversification)*

---

## selection-criteria

> "Just as natural selection favors certain traits, investors must develop criteria to select winning companies."

*(Chapter 5: Selection Criteria)*

---

## extinction-warning

> "Companies that fail to evolve face extinction, just as species that cannot adapt disappear from the earth."

*(Chapter 6: Corporate Extinction)*

---

## mutation-innovation

> "Innovation in business is like mutation in biology—it creates the variation needed for evolution."

*(Chapter 7: Innovation as Mutation)*

---

## ecosystem-thinking

> "Investors must understand the business ecosystem in which companies operate, just as biologists study ecosystems."

*(Chapter 8: Business Ecosystems)*

---

## fitness-landscape

> "The business landscape is constantly shifting. Companies must navigate this fitness landscape to survive and prosper."

*(Chapter 9: The Fitness Landscape)*

---

## patience-discipline

> "Darwin's theory teaches us patience. Evolution doesn't happen overnight, and neither do great investment returns."

*(Conclusion)*
