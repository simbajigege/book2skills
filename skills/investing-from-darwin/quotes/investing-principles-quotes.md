# What I Learned About Investing from Darwin — Investing Principles Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *What I Learned About Investing from Darwin* (Pulak Prasad, 2023).

---

## avoid-big-losses

> "The #1 rule in investing is to avoid big losses. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover."

*(Chapter 1: Oh, to Be a Bumblebee)*

---

## survival-before-thriving

> "Like evolution, survival comes before thriving."

*(Chapter 1: Oh, to Be a Bumblebee)*

---

## not-strongest-but-adaptable

> "Species that survive mass extinctions are not the strongest, but the most adaptable and risk-aware."

*(Chapter 1: Oh, to Be a Bumblebee)*

---

## quality-over-price

> "Look for companies with durable competitive advantages (moats) trading at fair prices, not cheap companies."

*(Chapter 2: The Siberian Solution)*

---

## darwin-ate-my-dcf

> "DCF models are unreliable because they require precise long-term forecasts. Evolution teaches that the future is inherently unpredictable — focus on robustness over precision."

*(Chapter 5: Darwin Ate My DCF)*

---

## replay-the-tape

> "If you replayed evolution, you'd get different outcomes. If you replayed business history, you'd also get different outcomes. Focus on robust businesses that can thrive in multiple scenarios."

*(Chapter 6: Bacteria and Business Replay the Tape)*

---

## be-very-lazy

> "Low portfolio turnover and long holding periods outperform frequent trading. Like evolution, change happens slowly — be very lazy as an investor."

*(Chapter 8: Birds and Bears Bare an Aberration)*

---

## punctuated-equilibrium

> "Punctuated equilibrium in evolution means long periods of stability interrupted by rapid change. In investing, most of the time you should do nothing, and act decisively during rare opportunities."

*(Chapter 9: Eldredge and Gould Dredge Up Investing Gold)*

---

## rare-opportunities

> "Great investment opportunities are rare. Like evolution's rare beneficial mutations, you must wait patiently for the right opportunities and act decisively when they appear."

*(Chapter 10: Where Are the Rabbits?)*

---

## three-rules-from-darwin

> "Three rules from Darwin for investing: 1) Avoid big risks, 2) Buy high quality at a fair price, 3) Don't be lazy — be very lazy."

*(Conclusion)*
