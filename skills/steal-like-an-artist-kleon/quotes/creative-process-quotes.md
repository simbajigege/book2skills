# Steal Like an Artist — Creative Process Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *Steal Like an Artist: 10 Things Nobody Told You About Being Creative* (Austin Kleon, 2012).

---

## nothing-is-original

> "What a good artist understands is that nothing comes from nowhere. All creative work builds on what came before. Nothing is completely original."

> "If we're free from the burden of trying to be completely original, we can stop trying to make something out of nothing, and we can embrace influence instead of running away from it."

*(1. Steal Like an Artist)*

---

## selective-collection

> "The artist is a collector. Not a hoarder, mind you, there's a difference: Hoarders collect indiscriminately, artists collect selectively. They only collect things that they really love."

> "Your job is to collect good ideas. The more good ideas you collect, the more you can choose from to be influenced by."

*(1. Steal Like an Artist)*

---

## imitation-to-emulation

> "At some point, you'll have to move from imitating your heroes to emulating them. Imitation is about copying. Emulation is when imitation goes one step further, breaking through into your own thing."

> "A wonderful flaw about human beings is that we're incapable of making perfect copies. Our failure to copy our heroes is where we discover where our own thing lives. That is how we evolve."

*(1. Steal Like an Artist)*

---

## fake-it-til-you-make-it

> "If I'd waited to know who I was or what I was about before I started 'being creative,' well, I'd still be sitting around trying to figure myself out instead of making things. In my experience, it's in the act of making things and doing our work that we figure out who we are."

> "You're ready. Start making stuff."

*(2. Don't Wait Until You Know Who You Are)*

---

## write-what-you-like

> "The best advice is not to write what you know, it's to write what you like. Write the kind of story you like best—write the story you want to read."

> "Draw the art you want to see, start the business you want to run, play the music you want to hear, write the books you want to read, build the products you want to use—do the work you want to see done."

*(3. Write the Book You Want to Read)*

---

## use-your-hands

> "Work that only comes from the head isn't any good."

> "We need to move, to feel like we're making something with our bodies, not just our heads."

*(4. Use Your Hands)*

---

## side-projects-matter

> "The work you do while you procrastinate is probably the work you should be doing for the rest of your life."

*(5. Side Projects and Hobbies Are Important)*

---

## do-good-work-and-share

> "If there was a secret formula for becoming known, I would give it to you. But there's only one not-so-secret formula that I know: Do good work and share it with people."

> "Step 1: Wonder at something. Step 2: Invite others to wonder with you."

*(6. The Secret)*

---

## constraints-breed-creativity

> "The way to get over creative block is to simply place some constraints on yourself. It seems contradictory, but when it comes to creative work, limitations mean freedom."

> "In the end, creativity isn't just the things we choose to put in, it's the things we choose to leave out."

*(10. Creativity Is Subtraction)*

---

## be-regular-be-boring

> "Be regular and orderly in your life, so that you may be violent and original in your work."

*(9. Be Boring, quoting Flaubert)*

---

## enjoy-obscurity

> "Enjoy your obscurity while it lasts. Use it."

> "There's no pressure when you're unknown. You can do what you want. Experiment. Do things just for the fun of it. When you're unknown, there's nothing to distract you from getting better."

*(6. The Secret)*
