# The Little Book of Common Sense Investing — Cost Matters Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Little Book of Common Sense Investing* (John C. Bogle, 2017).

---

## tyranny-of-costs

> "The magic of compounding returns, the tyranny of compounding costs — over the long term, costs overwhelm returns."

*(Chapter 4: The Cost Matters Hypothesis)*

---

## two-percent-costs

> "Even if the cost of financial market activity totals as little as 2 percent a year, its long-term impact is huge."

*(Chapter 4: The Cost Matters Hypothesis)*

---

## taxes-are-costs

> "High-turnover funds generate large tax bills. Index funds are highly tax-efficient due to low turnover."

*(Chapter 8: Taxes Are Costs, Too)*

---

## past-performance-no-guarantee

> "Past performance does not predict future results. Almost no funds consistently beat the market over long periods."

*(Chapter 10: Selecting Long-Term Winners)*

---

## bond-costs-even-worse

> "The same cost principles apply to bond funds. Costs eat even more of bond returns since bond returns are lower."

*(Chapter 14: Bond Funds)*

---

## speculative-return

> "Investment return + speculative return = total return. Speculative return is unpredictable and typically mean-reverting."

*(Chapter 9: When the Good Times No Longer Roll)*

---

## survivorship-bias

> "The illusion of beating the market is sustained by survivorship bias and selective reporting. Most funds that underperform simply disappear."

*(Chapter 7: The Grand Illusion)*

---

## etf-trading-danger

> "ETFs offer index investing but with trading flexibility that encourages harmful speculation. Traditional index funds remain superior for long-term investors."

*(Chapter 15: The ETF)*

---

## graham-margin-of-safety

> "Graham's margin of safety principle supports indexing — low costs provide the margin of safety."

*(Chapter 17: Benjamin Graham and Indexing)*
