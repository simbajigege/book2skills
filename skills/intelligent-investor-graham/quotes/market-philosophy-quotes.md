# The Intelligent Investor — Market Philosophy Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Intelligent Investor* (Benjamin Graham).

---

## market-is-a-pendulum

> "The market is a pendulum that forever swings between unsustainable optimism (which makes stocks too expensive) and unjustified pessimism (which makes them too cheap). The intelligent investor is a realist who sells to optimists and buys from pessimists."

—Benjamin Graham's core principles

*(A Note About Benjamin Graham)*

---

## earnings-can-be-manipulated

> "Earnings can be manipulated. Look for consistent earnings over time, not one-time gains or creative accounting."

*(Chapter 12: Per-Share Earnings)*

---

## avoid-ipos

> "The enterprising investor should avoid: IPOs, aggressively marketed securities, and any investment that sounds too good to be true."

*(Chapter 6: Negative Approach)*

---

## index-funds-best

> "Most funds underperform the market. Index funds are the best choice for the defensive investor."

*(Chapter 9: Investing in Investment Funds)*

---

## advisers-misaligned

> "Be wary of investment advisers who claim to beat the market. Their incentives are not aligned with yours."

*(Chapter 10: The Investor and His Advisers)*

---

## net-current-asset-bargains

> "Look for stocks selling below their net current asset value — the ultimate bargain."

*(Chapter 15: Stock Selection for the Enterprising Investor)*

---

## simplicity-beats-cleverness

> "Simplicity and discipline beat complexity and cleverness."

*(Throughout)*

---

## santayana-warning

> "No statement is more true and better applicable to Wall Street than the famous warning of Santayana: 'Those who do not remember the past are condemned to repeat it.'"

*(Introduction)*
