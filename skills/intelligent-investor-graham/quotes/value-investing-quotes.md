# The Intelligent Investor — Value Investing Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Intelligent Investor* (Benjamin Graham, revised by Jason Zweig).

---

## buffett-endorses-graham

> "To invest successfully over a lifetime does not require a stratospheric IQ, unusual business insights, or inside information. What's needed is a sound intellectual framework for making decisions and the ability to keep emotions from corroding that framework."

—Warren Buffett, Preface to the Fourth Edition

*(Preface)*

---

## investment-vs-speculation

> "An investment operation is one which, upon thorough analysis, promises safety of principal and an adequate return. Operations not meeting these requirements are speculative."

*(Chapter 1: Investment versus Speculation)*

---

## margin-of-safety

> "The margin of safety is the secret of sound investing. Never overpay, no matter how exciting an investment seems."

*(Chapter 20: Margin of Safety)*

---

## mr-market

> "Imagine that in some private business you own a small share that cost you $1,000. One of your partners, named Mr. Market, is very obliging indeed. Every day he tells you what he thinks your interest is worth and furthermore offers either to buy you out or to sell you an additional interest on that basis."

*(Chapter 8: The Investor and Market Fluctuations)*

---

## mr-market-servant

> "The market is a pendulum — Mr. Market is your servant, not your master. Buy when Mr. Market is pessimistic, sell when he's optimistic."

*(Chapter 8: The Investor and Market Fluctuations)*

---

## defensive-portfolio-split

> "The defensive investor should divide funds between high-grade bonds and leading common stocks — never less than 25% in stocks and never more than 75%."

*(Chapter 4: General Portfolio Policy)*

---

## price-matters-more

> "Comparing pairs of similar companies reveals that price matters enormously — the cheaper company almost always outperforms."

*(Chapter 18: Comparison of Eight Pairs)*

---

## graham-core-principles

> "A stock is not just a ticker symbol or an electronic blip; it is an ownership interest in an actual business, with an underlying value that does not depend on its share price."

—Benjamin Graham's core principles, as summarized by Jason Zweig

*(A Note About Benjamin Graham)*

---

## the-future-value-depends-on-price

> "The future value of every investment is a function of its present price. The higher the price you pay, the lower your return will be."

—Benjamin Graham's core principles

*(A Note About Benjamin Graham)*

---

## no-need-for-extraordinary

> "In investing it is not necessary to do extraordinary things to get extraordinary results."

—Warren Buffett, citing Graham

*(Chapter 20: Margin of Safety)*
