# Building a StoryBrand — Main Framework Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *Building a StoryBrand: Clarify Your Message So Customers Will Listen* (Donald Miller, 2025).

---

## words-sell-things

> "Pretty websites don't sell things. Words sell things. And if we haven't clarified our message, our customers won't listen."

*(Chapter 1: The Key to Being Seen, Heard, and Understood)*

---

## brain-drawn-to-clarity

> "The human brain, no matter what region of the world it comes from, is drawn toward clarity and away from confusion."

*(Chapter 1: The Key to Being Seen, Heard, and Understood)*

---

## story-greatest-weapon

> "Story is the greatest weapon you have to combat noise because it organizes information in such a way that people are compelled to listen."

*(Chapter 2: The Secret Weapon That Will Grow Your Business)*

---

## customer-is-hero

> "The customer is the hero, not your brand."

*(Chapter 3: The Simple SB7 Framework)*

---

## customer-looks-for-guide

> "Customers aren't looking for another hero; they're looking for a guide."

*(Chapter 3 & 6: The Simple SB7 Framework / And Meets a Guide)*

## companies-sell-external

> "Companies tend to sell solutions to external problems, but customers buy solutions to internal problems."

*(Chapter 5: Has a Problem)*

---

## trust-guide-with-plan

> "Customers trust a guide who gives them a plan."

*(Chapter 3 & 7: The Simple SB7 Framework / Who Gives Them a Plan)*

## call-to-action-required

> "Customers don't take action unless challenged to take action."

*(Chapter 8: And Calls Them to Action)*

## avoid-tragic-ending

*(Chapter 9: That Helps Them Avoid Failure)*

## buying-transformation

> "Customers aren't just buying products; they're buying transformation."

*(Chapter 11: People Want Your Brand to Participate in Their Transformation)*

## if-you-confuse-you-lose

*(Chapter 12: Define Your Controlling Idea)*
