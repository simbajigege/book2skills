# The War of Art — Turning Pro & Inspiration Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The War of Art: Winning the Inner Creative Battle* (Steven Pressfield, 2002).

---

## secret-of-writing

> "There's a secret that real writers know that wannabe writers don't, and the secret is this: It's not the writing part that's hard. What's hard is sitting down to write."

> "What keeps us from sitting down is Resistance."

*(What I Know)*

---

## overcoming-resistance-daily

> "All that matters is I've put in my time and hit it with all I've got. All that counts is that, for this day, for this session, I have overcome Resistance."

*(What I Do)*

---

## professional-vs-amateur

> "The professional conducts his enterprise in the spirit of the great marshal, the field commander who, though he knows not what conditions await him on the far shore, has nevertheless prepared himself for every eventuality and is ready to commit his forces the moment he hits the beach."

> "The professional has learned that success, like happiness, comes as a by-product of work. The professional concentrates on the work and allows rewards to come or not come, whatever they like."

*(A Professional)*

---

## professional-acts-despite-fear

> "The amateur believes he must first overcome his fear; then he can do his work. The professional knows that fear can never be overcome. He knows there's no such thing as a fearless warrior or a dread-free artist."

> "What Henry Fonda does, after puking into the toilet in his dressing room, is to clean up and march out onstage anyway. That's the professional."

*(A Professional Acts in the Face of Fear)*

---

## professional-accepts-no-excuses

> "The professional accepts no excuses. He knows that when he schedules his work, he is going to suffer. He knows that Resistance will try to get him to rationalize that he doesn't have to do it today, or that he can put it off until tomorrow."

*(A Professional Accepts No Excuses)*

---

## professional-demystifies

> "The professional demystifies. He strips away the aura of magic that surrounds the creative process. He knows that the magic is not the act itself but the preparation for it."

*(A Professional Demystifies)*

---

## power-concentrates

> "When we sit down each day and do our work, power concentrates around us. The Muse takes her seat and attends to our labors. We become like a magnetized rod that attracts iron filings. Ideas come. Insights accrete."

*(Invoking the Muse)*

---

## magic-of-making-start

> "The most important thing about art is to work. Nothing else matters except sitting down every day and trying."

*(The Magic of Making a Start)*

---

## territory-vs-hierarchy

> "A territory can be a real physical place or it can be a field of study or a state of mind. It may be a piece of land, a genre of writing, a body of knowledge, or a set of principles. The defining characteristic of a territory is that it provides the artist with sustenance; it replenishes and renews."

> "The territory provides the artist with all that he needs and nothing that he doesn't. It gives him a sense of home and a feeling of being in the right place."

*(The Territorial Orientation)*

---

## definition-of-a-hack

> "A hack is a writer who second-guesses his audience. When a hack writes, the question he asks is not 'Is this what I want to say?' but 'Is this what the audience wants to hear?'"

*(The Definition of a Hack)*

---

## supreme-virtue

> "The supreme virtue in art is the capacity to face the grind. To endure the tedium. To stare down the abyss of nothingness and keep working."

*(The Supreme Virtue)*

---

## this-moment-change

> "Never forget: This very moment, we can change our lives. There never was a moment, and never will be, when we are without the power to alter our destiny. This second, we can turn the tables on Resistance."

> "This second, we can sit down and do our work."

*(Resistance and Procrastination, Part Two)*
