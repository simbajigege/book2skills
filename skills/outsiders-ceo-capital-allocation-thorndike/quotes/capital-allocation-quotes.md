# The Outsiders — Capital Allocation Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional CEOs and Their Radically Rational Blueprint for Success* (William N. Thorndike, Jr., 2012).

---

## capital-allocation-most-important-job

> "Capital allocation is a CEO's most important job."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## per-share-value-not-growth

> "What counts in the long run is the increase in per share value, not overall growth or size."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## cash-flow-not-earnings

> "Cash flow, not reported earnings, is what determines long-term value."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## decentralized-organizations

> "Decentralized organizations release entrepreneurial energy and keep both costs and 'rancor' down."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## independent-thinking-essential

> "Independent thinking is essential to long-term success, and interactions with outside advisers (Wall Street, the press, etc.) can be distracting and time-consuming."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## best-investment-your-own-stock

> "Sometimes the best investment opportunity is your own stock."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## patience-and-boldness

> "With acquisitions, patience is a virtue . . . as is occasional boldness."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## two-companies-different-outcomes

> "Two companies with identical operating results and different approaches to allocating capital will derive two very different long-term outcomes for shareholders."

*(Introduction — An Intelligent Iconoclasm)*

---

## singleton-over-welch

> "He wasn't even in the same zip code as Henry Singleton, however."

*(Preface — Singletonville, on Jack Welch)*

---

## ceo-measurement

> "You really only need to know three things to evaluate a CEO's greatness: the compound annual return to shareholders during his or her tenure and the return over the same period for peer companies and for the broader market."

*(Preface — Singletonville)*

---

## always-do-the-math

> "Always do the math."

*(Epilogue — The Outsider's Checklist)*

## denominator-matters

> "The denominator matters."

*(Epilogue — The Outsider's Checklist, on per-share value)*
