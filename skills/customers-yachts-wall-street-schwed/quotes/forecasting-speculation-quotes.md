# Where Are the Customers' Yachts? - Forecasting and Speculation Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *Where Are the Customers' Yachts?* (Fred Schwed Jr.).

---

## difficult-answer-dont-know

> "Rarely will it be the most difficult of all answers - I don't know."

*(The Passion for Prophecy)*

---

## prediction-loses-savings

> "It is an easy step for him to go downtown and start telling people what United States Steel is going to do month after next. That is liable to lose someone's life savings for him."

*(The Passion for Prophecy)*

---

## just-a-croupier

> "The croupier at the roulette table does not claim that he knows something about the order in which the numbers will come up."

*(The Passion for Prophecy)*

---

## charts-useful-accuracy

> "This writer does not believe that they can predict the future with any useful accuracy."

*(Chartists)*

---

## probability-and-risk

> "Wall Street has always been burdened by having in its personnel a good many otherwise estimable people who don't know anything about the laws of probability and risk, and not too much about arithmetic."

*(A Little Aptitude Test)*

---

## buy-boom-sell-depression

> "When there is a stock-market boom, and everyone is scrambling for common stocks, take all your common stocks and sell them."

*(Little Wonderful Advice)*

---

## panic-buy-stocks

> "When this depression - or panic - becomes a national catastrophe, sell out the bonds and buy back the stocks."

*(Little Wonderful Advice)*

---

## dying-rich

> "Continue to repeat this operation as long as you live, and you'll have the pleasure of dying rich."

*(Little Wonderful Advice)*
