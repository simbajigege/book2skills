# Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook — Framework Quotes

Verbatim excerpts from *Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook: How to Tell Your Story in a Noisy Social World* (Gary Vaynerchuk, 2013).

---

## jab-jab-jab-right-hook-metaphor

> "There's a reason why boxing is called 'the sweet science.' The right hook gets all the credit for the win, but it's the ring movement and the series of well-planned jabs that come before it that set you up for success."

*(Introduction: Weigh-In)*

---

## jab-definition

> "Jabs are the one conversation, one engagement at a time that slowly but authentically builds relationships between brands and customers."

*(Introduction: Weigh-In)*

---

## right-hook-definition

> "Right hooks convert traffic to sales. Right hooks earn Cannes Cyber Lions Awards. They easily show results and ROI."

*(Introduction: Weigh-In)*

---

## native-content-essential

> "Native storytelling—authentic content perfectly crafted for that particular new platform."

*(Introduction: Weigh-In)*

---

## number-one-job

> "Your number-one job is to tell your story to the consumer wherever they are, and preferably at the moment they are deciding to make a purchase."

*(Introduction: Weigh-In)*

---

## social-media-best-chance

> "Social media platforms offer us our best chance to stretch our working dollars the furthest."

*(Introduction: Weigh-In)*

---

## native-content-definition

> "Native content is content that is perfectly crafted for the platform where it appears. It fits the platform's tone and offers the same value as the other content that people come to the platform to consume."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*

---

## story-non-intrusive

> "A story is at its best when it's not intrusive, when it brings value to a platform's consumers, and when it fits in as a natural step along the customer's path to making a purchase."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*

---

## brand-identity-consistency

> "Native storytelling doesn't require you to alter your identity to suit a given platform; your identity remains the same no matter what."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*

---

## sweet-science-marketing

> "Marketers are constantly asking me for a fixed storytelling blueprint. That blueprint doesn't exist. Social media storytelling is as sweet a science as boxing, requiring constant experimentation and hours of observation."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*

---

## platform-uniqueness

> "Each platform is unique, and requires a unique formula. What works on Facebook won't necessarily work on Twitter."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*

---

## perfect-right-hook-three-characteristics

> "Today's perfect right hooks always include three characteristics: 1) They make the call to action simple and easy to understand. 2) They are perfectly crafted for mobile, as well as all digital devices. 3) They respect the nuances of the social network for which you are making the content."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*

---

## platform-native-language

> "Getting people to hear your story on social media, and then act on it, requires using a platform's native language, paying attention to context, understanding the nuances and subtle differences that make each platform unique, and adapting your content to match."

*(Round 1: The Setup)*
