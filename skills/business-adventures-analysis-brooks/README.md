# Business Adventures Analysis (John Brooks)

A Claude skill that applies the analytical frameworks from John Brooks' *Business Adventures: Twelve Classic Tales from the World of Wall Street* to real business situations. Distilled from 12 landmark case studies — the 1962 market crash, the Edsel failure, the GE price-fixing scandal, the Texas Gulf Sulphur insider trading case, the rise of Xerox, and more — this skill helps you recognize the timeless patterns behind corporate failures, market crises, and business successes.

Warren Buffett called *Business Adventures* the best business book he ever read. This skill puts its frameworks at your fingertips.

## What It Does

- **Diagnoses corporate and product failures** against 6 historical pattern dimensions
- **Analyzes market panics and crises** using the panic-arc framework from the 1962 crash
- **Assesses regulatory and legal risk** using the Texas Gulf Sulphur materiality standard
- **Evaluates innovation claims** against the Xerox disruption template
- **Identifies corporate communication failures** using the GE three-layer breakdown model
- **Flags governance theater** vs. genuine accountability mechanisms

## When to Use It

Trigger this skill with phrases like:
- *"Why did [company/product] fail?"*
- *"What does this situation remind you of historically?"*
- *"Analyze this business case"*
- *"What patterns apply here?"*
- *"What would John Brooks say about [current event]?"*
- *"Is this a warning sign?"*
- *"How do market panics typically unfold?"*
- *"Lessons from [Edsel / Xerox / GE / Texas Gulf / 1962 crash]"*
