# Skill: Governance Accountability (Business Adventures — Module 5)

**Knowledge source:** *Business Adventures* by John Brooks  
**Reference library:** `references/case_library.md`  
**Standalone use:** This module can run independently or as part of `../../SKILL.md`.

## When to Use

Use this module for board accountability, shareholder power, annual meetings, executive incentives, crisis response, ethics codes, committees, or cases where formal governance may not create real consequences.

## Module Inputs

- Governance body and decision being assessed
- Board, executive, shareholder, regulator, and stakeholder roles
- Incentives and consequences for decision-makers
- Crisis response, disclosure, and remediation actions

## Module Outputs

- Governance theater vs real accountability diagnosis
- Power map of who can impose consequences
- Incentive and consequence gap
- Governance action recommendation

## Execution Flow

### Overview

```text
Governance accountability
├── Step 1: Map formal authority
├── Step 2: Map actual power
├── Step 3: Test consequence quality
├── Step 4: Diagnose dissent handling
└── Step 5: Recommend accountability mechanism
```

### Step 1: Map Formal Authority

**Core question:** What formal governance mechanism is supposed to control the behavior?

**Decision rules:**

- Annual meetings, votes, committees, and codes are weak unless they alter incentives.
- Board independence matters only if independent actors can impose consequences.
- Public statements are not remediation unless operating behavior changes.

**Ask the user:** What actual consequence followed the governance process?

**Citation instruction:** Cite `REF-M5-001` or `REF-M5-002` for shareholder power and meeting authority.

### Step 2: Map Actual Power

**Core question:** Who can change rules, budgets, leadership, incentives, or disclosure?

**Decision rules:**

| Formal sign | Reviewer question |
|---|---|
| Committee formed | Did it change incentives or only optics? |
| Ethics code cited | Did frontline metrics change? |
| Shareholders spoke | Could they alter outcomes? |
| Leadership apologized | Did accountable people lose power, pay, or role? |

**Citation instruction:** Cite `REF-M5-003` when corporate power overwhelms formal participation.

### Step 3: Test Consequence Quality

**Core question:** Are losses absorbed by the institution while decision-makers keep upside or status?

**Decision rules:**

- Accountability is strong when decision-makers bear direct consequences.
- Accountability is weak when blame is pushed down to executors while incentives remain.
- Crisis fixes are credible only if they alter recurring behavior.

**Citation instruction:** Cite `REF-M5-004` for institutional absorption of losses or `REF-M5-005` for other-people's-money dynamics.

## Do Not

- Do not equate board process with accountability.
- Do not stop at public statements or committee formation.
- Do not ignore who keeps upside after the institution absorbs downside.
