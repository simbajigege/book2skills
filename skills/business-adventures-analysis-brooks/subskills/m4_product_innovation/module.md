# Skill: Product and Innovation Review (Business Adventures — Module 4)

**Knowledge source:** *Business Adventures* by John Brooks  
**Reference library:** `references/case_library.md`  
**Standalone use:** This module can run independently or as part of `../../SKILL.md`.

## When to Use

Use this module for product launches, failed products, product naming, market research, timing, incumbent blindness, invention, commercialization, or Xerox/Edsel-style comparisons.

## Module Inputs

- Product or innovation being assessed
- Target customer and use case
- Research evidence and date of research
- Launch timing, distribution, and internal commitments
- Incumbent incentives and likely cannibalization

## Module Outputs

- Product timing and market-fit diagnosis
- Innovation type: sustaining, new-market, or unclear
- Incumbent-blindness assessment
- Stop/redesign/accelerate/monitor recommendation

## Execution Flow

### Overview

```text
Product and innovation review
├── Step 1: Test current market fit
├── Step 2: Check timing drift
├── Step 3: Evaluate differentiation and naming
├── Step 4: Diagnose incumbent blindness
└── Step 5: Recommend action
```

### Step 1: Test Current Market Fit

**Core question:** Does current buyer behavior still match the assumptions used to design the product?

**Decision rules:**

- Old research is weak evidence after a market shift.
- Stated preference is weaker than observed buying behavior.
- A committee compromise often creates a product no one strongly wants.

**Ask the user:** What has changed since the product was planned?

**Citation instruction:** Cite `REF-M4-001` for Edsel research risk.

### Step 2: Check Timing Drift

**Core question:** Is launch timing driven by customer readiness or internal commitments?

**Decision rules:**

| Signal | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Launch continues despite weaker evidence | Sunk-cost timing drift |
| Category is moving away from the design | Market shifted underneath planning |
| Distribution complies but lacks conviction | Campaign commitment is masking product doubt |

**Citation instruction:** Cite `REF-M4-002` or `REF-M4-003` when timing and category mismatch matter.

### Step 3: Diagnose Innovation Dynamics

**Core question:** Is the innovation creating a new market that incumbents cannot measure with old tools?

**Decision rules:**

- Incumbents may reject a market that threatens existing margins.
- Early unexpected users can be more informative than conventional market-size estimates.
- Invention is not enough; commercialization, service, distribution, and organizational scaling matter.

**Citation instruction:** Cite `REF-M4-004` and `REF-M4-005` for Xerox-style innovation and complacency.

## Do Not

- Do not treat extensive planning as proof of market fit.
- Do not use old market research after the market has changed.
- Do not confuse technical invention with durable commercialization.
